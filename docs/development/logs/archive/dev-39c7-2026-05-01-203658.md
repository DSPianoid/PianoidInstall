**Revision B: horizontal stretch scales BOTH `mu` AND `sigma` per cell.**

The user's mathematical interpretation is uniform horizontal scaling of the Gauss curve around the origin — `f(t) = volume · exp(−(t − μ)² / (2σ²))` scales horizontally by factor `k` ⟹ both `μ` and `σ` scale together:

  - `μ_new = μ_anchor · k`
  - `σ_new = σ_anchor · k` (same k as μ)

This matters because scaling `μ` alone shifts the centre but leaves the curve's width unchanged — the Gauss bump just slides along the t-axis. Scaling `μ` AND `σ` together stretches the entire curve uniformly, which is what "stretch horizontally" intuitively means visually.

**Vertical stretch unchanged** — still `volume × k` per cell, 25 changes per click.
